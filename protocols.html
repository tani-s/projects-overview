    • Protocols App
    • This app started as an idea in response to a prompt in my Design Thinking and Doing class. Here, you can see my design process and progress. I've arrived at a wireframe, and plan on coming back to this project as a way of practicing new skills.

      Skip to wireframe

    • Design Prompt
      • Masks and social norms in the era of COVID-19
      • "People are confused about how to live with masks. Tensions abound in scenarios like eating and drinking, exercising, and crossing city and state boundaries. We make strong impressions on others by choosing to wear, or not wear, one in certain situations. How can we establish safe and practical norms for this brand-new societal practice?"
      • Honing a "How might we..."
      • This prompt got my group thinking — not just about mask wearing, but about COVID norms in general. We can just come back to campus and everyone was feeling the tension of not having clear-cut social protocol. Roommates wondered how to see other friends or significant others, about how to measure the risk of a meal out or a ride on public transit. We came up with a more specific research question: How might we make it easier for roommates or "pods" to establish and maintain agreed upon COVID norms?
    • How might we...
    • Protocols App
      • Find it on the App Store.
      • I ended up drafting a couple images of what a "Protocols App" might look like. My group brainstormed different things we'd want to see in an app. Starting with the basics: a tool to agree upon norms, called "Protocols", with a group of people; resources on COVID testing and local rates of infection; maybe the ability to share test results with others... what about contact tracing?

        I fought for the inclusion of contact tracing in the app because I myself am a visual learner. I need to see something to understand it; I'd even sketched out a contact tracing network myself when I was thinking about seeing a friend who'd seen others since her last test. What if the app could do that for you? People might take social distancing a little more seriously.
      • From sketch to wireframe
      • After sketching out some ideas for the functionality of the app in OneNote, I went to Balsamiq to whip up a clickable prototype. Results below!
